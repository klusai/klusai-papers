\begin{abstract}
% POSITIONING DISCIPLINE: "first UNIFIED", never bare "first". re-id reserved for the national-ID channel.
% Synthetic numbers config_status=dev (KLU-27); TAB numbers real-external-gold, citable in scope. No SOTA.
\noindent\textbf{Draft / skeleton --- not for submission.} High aggregate detection scores are the
standard way de-identification systems are reported and trusted. We show that this aggregate
\emph{masks} the failures that matter most: the rare, high-stakes tokens that actually carry
re-identification. Using the EuroPriv-Bench leaderboard~\cite{europrivbench2026}, we present the
first \emph{unified} demonstration that detection entity-F1 and re-identification protection are
\emph{dissociated} --- a high-F1 detector can still leak the decode-bearing national identifiers
(Romanian CNP, Polish PESEL, Italian Codice Fiscale) through which a subject is provably
re-identified --- and we show the same dissociation survives on \emph{externally annotated, real
legal gold} (TAB ECHR). On the Romanian real-skeleton track, a strong general PII detector reaches
entity-F1 $0.85$ yet leaks $30.2\%$ of distinct CNP subjects; a span-only spaCy baseline at F1
$0.14$ leaks $89\%$; while an openly-released protector trained for the redaction objective leaks
$0\%$ at F1 $0.74$. The dissociation holds across three national-ID schemes in three languages
(RO/PL/IT) and across two Romanian template families, and is statistically significant under an
item-paired McNemar test (e.g.\ $p\!=\!1.79\!\times\!10^{-102}$ vs.\ the F1 leader). We reserve the
term \emph{re-identification} for this deterministic national-ID channel.

On \emph{real} external legal gold (TAB ECHR English; Pil\'an et al.\ 2022~\cite{pilan2022tab}) we
report a complementary, \emph{citable} result on a separate axis: a CJEU-structure-trained checkpoint
attains the lowest \emph{direct-identifier} leak rate, $0.095$ (the $95\%$ CI $[0.065,0.136]$ is the
Wilson interval on the $n{=}264$ DIRECT subjects, single seed; seed-robustness is reported
separately), versus
the next-best $0.496$ (spaCy) and $0.500$ (Presidio) --- a paired-bootstrap improvement of
$\Delta\!=\!-0.40$ ($95\%$ CI $[-0.477,-0.322]$, $n{=}264$ paired direct-identifier subjects),
3-seed confirmed. We frame this as \textbf{direct-identifier protection / re-identification-\emph{risk}
reduction} on real legal gold --- a broader axis than, and reported separately from, the deterministic
national-ID \emph{re-identification} channel above. We do \textbf{not} lead detection-F1 on TAB
(synthetic training caps at $0.340$ vs.\ Presidio $0.589$); the dissociation is the point. This TAB
checkpoint is \textbf{openly released} as \texttt{klusai/kp-deid-xlmr-560m-legal}; the openly
released \texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m} protector is a \emph{different} (280M) model evaluated on the
national-ID tracks --- both models are now public, and we keep them crisply distinct.

We separately report a \textbf{preliminary, in-progress} second mechanism --- a name-in-context
residual leak --- as corroborating evidence of \emph{residual distinctiveness} (not
re-identification), and frame the quasi-identifier-combination metric as future work. We release the
\texttt{kp-deid} protector and the analysis harness. \sotagate{} The synthetic real-skeleton results
are \texttt{config\_status=dev} (a finding, gated on KLU-27); the TAB direct-identifier results are on
real external gold and are citable within their stated scope (single board, single architecture,
TAB-EN-legal).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% THESIS (one sentence): aggregate detection-F1 masks failures on the rare, high-stakes tokens that
% carry re-identification; national IDs are the clearest provable case, not the whole of it.
\textbf{Thesis.} \emph{Aggregate detection-F1 masks failures on the rare, high-stakes tokens that
carry re-identification: national IDs are the clearest provable case --- deterministic and
decode-bearing --- but not the whole of it.}

De-identification systems are routinely ranked by an aggregate detection metric (entity-level F1 or
recall). We argue this is the wrong target for a \emph{privacy} guarantee. A single missed
decode-bearing national identifier (e.g.\ a Romanian CNP, which encodes date of birth, sex, and
county of registration) re-identifies a subject regardless of how many low-stakes spans were caught.
Because such identifiers are rare relative to the token mass, a model can post a high aggregate F1
while still leaking them at a high rate.

This is, to our knowledge, the first \emph{unified} demonstration --- across three national-ID
schemes, three languages, and two template families on one reproducible leaderboard, \emph{and on
externally annotated real legal gold (TAB ECHR)} --- that detection accuracy and re-identification
protection are \emph{dissociated} (we claim ``first \emph{unified}'', not ``first'': the individual
ingredients exist in prior art, but no prior artifact combines them; Section~\ref{sec:related}, and
re-verify via the KLU-15 rescan before any public claim). We make the dissociation visual (a Pareto
frontier, Figure~\ref{fig:pareto}), statistically significant (item-paired McNemar on the synthetic
tracks; a paired bootstrap on the real TAB gold), and actionable (an openly-released protector that
escapes the leak axis on the national-ID tracks, and a structure-trained protector that leads the
direct-identifier-leak axis on real legal gold). \emph{Re-identification} is used \textbf{only} for
the deterministic national-ID channel; the TAB DIRECT axis is reported as
\emph{direct-identifier protection} that bounds re-identification \emph{risk}; all other channels are
framed as residual distinctiveness.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item A unified \emph{detection $\neq$ re-identification} dissociation grounded in public
        leaderboard numbers across RO/PL/IT national-ID schemes and two RO template families
        (Section~\ref{sec:dissociation}).
  \item An item-paired significance test (McNemar) on per-subject national-ID protection, and a
        Pareto-frontier visualization of F1 vs.\ leak (Section~\ref{sec:dissociation},
        Figure~\ref{fig:pareto}).
  \item A \emph{real-gold confirmation on TAB ECHR}: the same dissociation holds on externally
        annotated legal gold, and a CJEU-structure-trained checkpoint
        (\texttt{kp-cjeu-structure}) leads the direct-identifier-leak (re-identification-\emph{risk})
        axis --- DIRECT-leak $0.095$ vs.\ runner-up $\approx\!0.50$, 3-seed robust and
        paired-bootstrap significant ($\Delta\!=\!-0.40$, $95\%$ CI $[-0.477,-0.322]$, $n{=}264$),
        zero-shot from a contamination-controlled CJEU real-\emph{structure} corpus
        (Section~\ref{sec:tab}). This checkpoint is \emph{openly released} as
        \texttt{klusai/kp-deid-xlmr-560m-legal} and is distinct from the (also public) protector
        of contribution~4; the latter ranks $7$th on this TAB axis and we never conflate
        the two.
  \item An openly-released protector (\texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m}) that sits \emph{off} the
        detection-optimal frontier on the national-ID tracks: $0\%$ national-ID leak at non-maximal
        F1 (Section~\ref{sec:protector}). ``The protector'' is therefore not a single model: the
        \texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m} model leads the national-ID axis, while a distinct, also
        openly-released protector (\texttt{klusai/kp-deid-xlmr-560m-legal}) leads the TAB
        direct-identifier axis.
  \item A \textbf{preliminary, in-progress} second mechanism (name-in-context residual leak) reported
        as corroborating evidence only, and a QI-combination metric stated as future work
        (Section~\ref{sec:corroborating}, Section~\ref{sec:futurework}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background and Threat Model}
\label{sec:threat}
% Two leak axes, two evidentiary bases. Axis 1 = deterministic national-ID re-identification on
% SYNTHETIC real-skeleton gold (config_status=dev, KLU-27 gate). Axis 2 = DIRECT/QUASI identifier
% leak on REAL TAB/ECHR external gold (config_status=real-external-gold, NOT under KLU-27). The same
% per-subject, post-detection-residual, leak-iff-any-occurrence machinery spans both, but the TAB
% axis is deliberately NOT relabelled re-identification.

\subsection{Two leak axes, two evidentiary bases}
\label{subsec:two-axes}
We measure identifier disclosure on the post-detection \emph{residual} (what a
de-identification model would leave un-redacted), never on raw text, along two distinct
axes that must not be conflated.

\paragraph{Axis 1 --- deterministic national-ID re-identification (synthetic gold).}
This is the channel for which we reserve the term \emph{re-identification}. A subject is one
distinct, checksum-valid national-ID value within one document, keyed by the tuple
$(\text{doc},\ \text{country},\ \text{normalized value})$ and deduplicated per KLU-49 (a CNP repeated
across the CNP and CASS fields is one subject). The subject is \emph{protected} iff \textbf{every}
occurrence is redacted and \emph{leaks} iff \textbf{any} occurrence survives; the leak-rate is the
fraction of distinct decode-bearing subjects left un-redacted, with a $95\%$ Wilson interval. A miss
is decode-bearing because the value deterministically discloses date-of-birth, sex, and county. This
metric runs on the \emph{synthetic} real-skeleton tracks (\texttt{ro/pl/it-realskeleton-v1}); the
identifiers, and therefore the leak counts, are generated, not drawn from a real population
(\texttt{config\_status=dev}, gated on KLU-27). We reuse the EuroPriv-Bench harmonized GDPR-aligned
taxonomy~\cite{europrivbench2026} rather than re-deriving it.

\paragraph{Axis 2 --- DIRECT/QUASI identifier leak on real legal gold (TAB).}
The axis introduced here is grounded in a \emph{real}, externally peer-reviewed,
human-annotated corpus: the Text Anonymization Benchmark
(TAB)~\cite{pilan2022tab}, $127$ European Court of Human Rights judgments remapped onto
the EuroPriv-Bench KP taxonomy (\texttt{tab-echr-legal-en-v1},
\texttt{config\_status=real-external-gold}). Because the annotations are externally
peer-reviewed human gold, this track does \textbf{not} depend on the program's own
native-speaker/IAA gate (KLU-27), which validates only \emph{our} synthetic skeletons.
We deliberately keep the label distinct from Axis~1: a leaked DIRECT/QUASI identifier is
a \emph{residual identifier disclosure}, not a deterministic decode, so we do not call it
re-identification. A lower direct-identifier leak rate means lower re-identification \emph{risk}.

\subsection{The TAB DIRECT/QUASI identifier-leak metric}
\label{subsec:tab-metric}
Every TAB gold span carries two annotations we exploit: an \emph{identifier type}
$\tau \in \{\textsc{direct}, \textsc{quasi}\}$ and a coreference \emph{entity id}.
\textsc{direct} spans are directly re-identifying on their own (e.g.\ a party name, a
case number); \textsc{quasi} spans re-identify only in combination (e.g.\ a date, an
address, an organisation).

\paragraph{Subject and leak unit.}
We define a \emph{subject} as one $(\text{doc},\ \text{entity\_id},\ \tau)$ triple. A
subject is composed of all gold mentions sharing that entity id within the document and
the same identifier type. Let $\mathrm{toks}(d)$ be the whitespace tokens of document
$d$ and let a model's per-token prediction be $\hat{y} \in \{\text{O}\} \cup
\text{(tagged)}$. A single occurrence (span) is \emph{detected} iff the model marks
\emph{any} of the whitespace tokens it overlaps as non-\texttt{O}:
\[
  \mathrm{det}(\text{span}) \;=\; \bigvee_{i\,:\, \mathrm{tok}_i \cap \text{span}\neq\varnothing}
  \big[\,\hat{y}_i \neq \text{O}\,\big].
\]
A \textbf{subject} is detected iff \emph{every} one of its occurrences is detected, and it
\emph{leaks} iff \emph{any} occurrence survives un-redacted:
\[
  \mathrm{det}(\text{subject}) \;=\; \bigwedge_{\text{span}\,\in\,\text{subject}}
  \mathrm{det}(\text{span}),
  \qquad
  \mathrm{leak}(\text{subject}) \;=\; \neg\,\mathrm{det}(\text{subject}).
\]
This is the same conjunctive ``leak iff any occurrence survives'' semantics used by the
national-ID axis, which makes the per-subject outcome flags directly comparable across the
two axes even though the underlying gold differs.

\paragraph{Per-group residual leak rate with Wilson CIs.}
Let $G \in \{\text{all},\ \textsc{direct},\ \textsc{quasi}\}$ partition the subjects.
For each group let $n_G$ be the number of distinct subjects and $\ell_G$ the number that
leak. The reported leak rate is $\hat{p}_G = \ell_G / n_G$ (lower is better), with a
two-sided $95\%$ \emph{Wilson score} interval
\[
  \frac{\hat{p}_G + \tfrac{z^2}{2 n_G} \;\pm\;
  z\sqrt{\tfrac{\hat{p}_G(1-\hat{p}_G)}{n_G} + \tfrac{z^2}{4 n_G^2}}}
  {1 + \tfrac{z^2}{n_G}},
  \qquad z = 1.95996.
\]
We use the Wilson interval rather than the Wald (normal-approximation) interval precisely
because it stays inside $[0,1]$ and remains well-calibrated for proportions near $0$ or
$1$ and for the modest $n$ of a $127$-document corpus --- the regime where a strong
protector's DIRECT leak rate is near zero and a Wald interval would extend below zero and
understate uncertainty. We report all three groups: the overall residual disclosure rate,
and the DIRECT and QUASI rates separately, because the privacy-relevant failure is
concentrated in the DIRECT group (a single leaked DIRECT identifier discloses a subject),
whereas QUASI leaks matter only in combination and are the natural bridge to the future
$k$-anonymity diagnostic (Section~\ref{sec:futurework}).

\paragraph{How this differs from the synthetic national-ID metric.}
The two metrics share the per-subject, post-detection-residual, leak-iff-any-occurrence
machinery and the Wilson-CI reporting, but differ in three load-bearing ways.
(i)~\textbf{Evidentiary basis.} The national-ID metric scores \emph{synthetic} subjects on
\texttt{dev} tracks; the TAB metric scores \emph{real}, externally peer-reviewed
human-annotated subjects (\texttt{real-external-gold}), so the TAB axis is not gated on
KLU-27. (ii)~\textbf{Subject key and disclosure model.} A national-ID subject is keyed by a
\emph{checksum-validated normalized value} and a miss is a \emph{deterministic decode}
(date-of-birth $+$ sex $+$ county), which is why we call it re-identification; a TAB
subject is keyed by an \emph{annotated coreference id} $+$ identifier type, and a miss is a
\emph{residual identifier disclosure} graded DIRECT vs.\ QUASI --- there is no decode, so it
is deliberately \emph{not} called re-identification. (iii)~\textbf{Grouping.} The
national-ID metric splits by \emph{decode-bearing} vs.\ \emph{coverage-only} family (and
never emits a re-id number for coverage-only IDs); the TAB metric splits by the
human-annotated DIRECT/QUASI sensitivity that the national-ID schemes do not carry.

\subsection{Paired-bootstrap significance protocol}
\label{subsec:paired-bootstrap}
The leak rate is a property of the \emph{same} fixed set of gold subjects scored under two
models, so comparisons must be \emph{paired}: an unpaired two-proportion test ignores that
the two models are evaluated on identical items and the discordance is what carries the
signal. For each model we dump per-subject outcomes
$\{(\text{doc},\ \text{entity\_id},\ \tau,\ \mathrm{detected})\}$ in a deterministic order
whose keys depend \emph{only} on the gold; the two dumps therefore align subject-for-subject
without any post-hoc matching.

Restricting to the privacy-critical DIRECT subjects, let
$\mathcal{S} = \{s_1,\dots,s_n\}$ be the subjects scored by \emph{both} models, and for
subject $s_k$ define the paired leak indicators
$a_k = \mathbb{1}[\text{model A leaks } s_k]$ and
$b_k = \mathbb{1}[\text{model B leaks } s_k]$. The observed statistic is the difference of
leak rates
\[
  \widehat{\Delta} \;=\; \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k \;-\; \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n} b_k,
\]
negative when model A (the protector) leaks fewer DIRECT identifiers. We bootstrap the CI
by resampling \emph{subjects} (not occurrences) with replacement $B = 2000$ times under a
fixed seed: each replicate draws indices $i_1,\dots,i_n$ uniformly from $\{1,\dots,n\}$,
recomputes $\Delta^{(b)} = \tfrac{1}{n}\sum_j a_{i_j} - \tfrac{1}{n}\sum_j b_{i_j}$, and the
$95\%$ percentile interval is $[\Delta^{(\lfloor 0.025B\rfloor)},
\Delta^{(\lceil 0.975B\rceil)}]$ over the sorted replicates. Resampling the paired
\emph{subjects} preserves the within-subject coupling, which is what makes the interval a
valid statement about the leak-rate difference rather than about two marginal proportions.
The protector leaks significantly fewer DIRECT identifiers iff the interval lies
\emph{entirely} below zero ($\Delta^{(\lceil 0.975B\rceil)} < 0$).

This complements the item-paired McNemar test used on the synthetic national-ID axis
(Section~\ref{sec:dissociation}): both are paired item-level tests on identical gold
subjects, but McNemar gives an exact two-sided $p$ on the discordant pairs of a $0\%$-vs.\
non-zero comparison, whereas the bootstrap yields a CI on the \emph{magnitude} of the
DIRECT leak-rate gap on the real TAB gold, where the protector's leak is small but not
necessarily exactly zero.

\subsection{Real-structure $+$ synthetic-PII training, with contamination control}
\label{subsec:cjeu-training}
\paragraph{Training signal (CJEU structure).}
The protector evaluated on the TAB/ECHR gold is trained on documents that reproduce the
\emph{structure} of CJEU-style legal instruments --- the genre, sectioning, citation and
boilerplate scaffolding --- while the personal data they carry is entirely
\emph{synthetic} (generated names, addresses, dates, and CNP-bearing fields). No source
text from any real corpus is redistributed or used as a label; only the document
\emph{shape} is faithful. This gives the model in-genre legal register without ingesting
real personal data.

\paragraph{Contamination control vs.\ the ECHR eval.}
The held-out evaluation is the \emph{real} ECHR gold (\texttt{tab-echr-legal-en-v1}), a
\textbf{different} European court (CJEU structure for training vs.\ ECHR documents for
evaluation), and the two share no documents, no annotations, and no source text. Under the
EuroPriv-Bench \texttt{contamination} policy each $(\text{model},\ \text{config})$ row
carries a marker; because no protector or baseline on the board was trained on the TAB/ECHR
data, every system --- ours included --- is marked \texttt{clean\_held\_out} on this track.
The CJEU-vs.-ECHR separation is the design choice that makes the TAB leak rate a fair
held-out measurement of in-genre legal de-identification rather than an artifact of
train/eval overlap; the protector never sees the eval corpus, the annotators, or even the
same court ($0$ document overlap, $0/4578$ TAB-sentence verbatim hits, RES-72). The training
data is synthetic-PII-only, so the protector also never trains on real personal data, and
the eval data is real-but-externally-annotated, so its gold does not pass through the
program's own KLU-27 gate. Consistent with the paper's claim-discipline, TAB/ECHR results
are reported as \texttt{config\_status=real-external-gold} findings and carry no SOTA
wording (\sotagate{}).

\section{The Detection $\neq$ Re-identification Dissociation}

\subsection{The headline anti-correlation (RO real-skeleton)}
On the Romanian real-skeleton track (\texttt{ro-realskeleton-v1}, $n{=}1123$ distinct CNP subjects),
detection F1 and CNP leak-rate move \emph{together}, not against each other, among content-NER
detectors. A strong general PII detector (GLiNER multi-pii) reaches entity-F1 $0.85$ yet leaks
$30.2\%$ of distinct CNP subjects; a span-only spaCy baseline at F1 $0.14$ leaks $89.0\%$; whereas
the kp-deid protector leaks $0\%$ at F1 $0.74$ --- it sits \emph{off} the detection-optimal frontier
(Figure~\ref{fig:pareto}, Table~\ref{tab:dissociation}). Higher detection accuracy did not buy
privacy protection.

\subsection{It holds across schemes, languages, and template families}
The dissociation is not a Romanian artifact. It reproduces on Polish PESEL (GLiNER F1 $0.82$,
leak $57.8\%$; kp-deid F1 $0.76$, leak $0\%$) and Italian Codice Fiscale (GLiNER F1 $0.86$, leak
$36.2\%$; kp-deid F1 $0.70$, leak $0\%$); see Table~\ref{tab:dissociation}. It also holds across two
independent Romanian template families (official correspondence; academic registry): a
difference-of-proportions test with Newcombe (1998) CIs shows the per-family gap CI excludes zero
for the typed content-NER detectors in both families (KLU-101 analysis artifact). This retires the
single-template-family caveat (KLU-114).

\subsection{Statistical significance (item-paired McNemar)}
We test per-subject national-ID protection with an item-paired McNemar test (each distinct gold
subject's national ID was either redacted or leaked; exact two-sided binomial $p$ on the discordant
pairs). On \texttt{ro-realskeleton-v1}, kp-deid vs.\ the F1 leader (GLiNER) gives discordant counts
$b{=}339$, $c{=}0$ ($p\!=\!1.79\!\times\!10^{-102}$): kp-deid protects subjects GLiNER leaks, never
the reverse. kp-deid vs.\ privacy-filter gives $b{=}16$, $c{=}0$ ($p\!=\!3.05\!\times\!10^{-5}$).
A genuine tie is reported honestly: kp-deid vs.\ Presidio gives $b{=}c{=}0$, $p{=}1$ (both reach
$0\%$ CNP leak on this track). Source: \texttt{analysis/mcnemar\_ro\_realskeleton.md} (KLU-53).

\section{The Dissociation on Real Legal Gold (TAB)}
\label{sec:tab}
% PRIMARY REAL-DATA EVIDENCE. The synthetic real-skeleton tracks (Section~\ref{sec:dissociation})
% establish the dissociation under contamination-controlled but synthetic conditions; this section
% shows it survives on externally annotated, REAL legal gold. Config: tab-echr-legal-en-v1.

The real-skeleton tracks (Section~\ref{sec:dissociation}) establish the dissociation under
contamination-controlled but synthetic conditions. We now show it survives on \emph{externally
annotated, real legal gold}: the Text Anonymization Benchmark (TAB)~\cite{pilan2022tab}, drawn from
European Court of Human Rights (ECHR) judgments. TAB ships its own human DIRECT/QUASI identifier
annotations, so the re-identification-\emph{risk} axis is defined by an external party, not by us. We
score it with \texttt{tab\_reid\_leakage} (Section~\ref{subsec:tab-metric}): the per-subject fraction
of TAB-annotated DIRECT identifiers left un-redacted on the post-detection residual (lower is safer);
on this board, a \emph{direct-identifier leak} denotes leaving such a DIRECT identifier in the
residual ($n{=}264$ distinct DIRECT subjects; $3{,}701$ QUASI subjects). We call this the
\emph{re-identification-risk axis} (a lower direct-identifier leak means lower re-identification
risk), but the measured quantity is a \emph{direct-identifier leak rate}, not a re-identification
rate --- the latter is reserved for the deterministic national-ID channel. Source:
\texttt{europriv-bench/analysis/tab\_reid\_leaderboard.json} (config \texttt{tab-echr-legal-en-v1}).

\subsection{Detection-F1 and leak are dissociated on real data too}
\label{sec:tab-dissociation}
The anti-correlation that holds on synthetic real-skeleton survives on TAB. The \emph{highest}
detection-F1 system, Presidio ($0.589$), is only the $3$rd-safest ($50.0\%$ DIRECT-leak); the second
strongest detector, GLiNER2-base (F1 $0.545$), is the $6$th-safest ($65.1\%$ leak). Conversely the
\#1-on-privacy \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} sits at detection-F1 $0.340$ ($5$th on detection) yet leaks
$5\times$ fewer DIRECT identifiers than anything above it. Strict entity-F1 penalises span-boundary
and entity-type disagreement that does not matter for privacy, while the re-id-risk metric rewards
what does --- not \emph{leaving identifiers behind}. On externally annotated real legal text, ranking
by detection accuracy and ranking by direct-identifier protection produce essentially \emph{different}
orderings: the dissociation is not an artifact of synthetic data.

\subsection{The kp-cjeu-structure \#1: 3-seed robust and paired-bootstrap significant}
\label{sec:tab-significance}
The \#1 result is not a single-seed artifact and the gap to the runner-up is statistically
significant under an item-paired test.

\textbf{Seed robustness.} Across three seeds $(0,1,2)$, DIRECT-leak is
$\{0.0947, 0.0985, 0.0985\}$ (mean $0.097$, range $0.004$) --- a spread two orders of magnitude
below the gap to spaCy ($0.496$). Detection-F1 across the same seeds is $\{0.340, 0.309, 0.317\}$.
(Source: \texttt{res104-reid-win-scorecard.md}.)

\textbf{Paired-bootstrap $\Delta$ CI.} On the $264$ DIRECT subjects shared with the runner-up
(spaCy), the per-subject DIRECT-leak difference is
$\Delta(\text{kp}-\text{spacy}) = -0.40$ ($0.0947$ vs.\ $0.4962$), $95\%$ CI $[-0.477, -0.322]$ over
$2{,}000$ resamples --- fully below zero, so \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} leaks significantly fewer
DIRECT identifiers than the next-best system. (Source:
\texttt{europriv-bench/analysis/reid\_paired\_bootstrap.json}.) Combined with the seed robustness,
this makes the \#1 a defensible claim scoped to TAB English legal gold.

\subsection{The win is not over-tagging}
\label{sec:tab-not-overtagging}
A trivial way to drive leak toward zero is to redact everything; \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} does
\emph{not} do this. On TAB it predicts non-\texttt{O} (would-redact) tokens at $9.1\%$, \emph{below}
the gold non-\texttt{O} rate of $11.3\%$ --- it under-tags overall rather than blanket-redacting.
Its over-redaction rate (false-positive redacted tokens) is $4.3\%$, a low utility cost. So the low
leak comes from efficiently touching the \emph{right} identifier tokens, not from carpet redaction;
this is precisely why its strict detection-F1 is only $0.340$ (boundary/type disagreement) while its
direct-identifier leak is best by far. (Source: \texttt{tab\_reid\_leaderboard.json},
\texttt{res104-reid-win-scorecard.md}.)

\subsection{The synthesis ceiling on detection-F1 (we do not lead detection)}
\label{sec:tab-synthesis-ceiling}
We concede the negative explicitly. On TAB \emph{detection}-F1, synthetic training caps below
Presidio's $0.589$: a generic-synthetic recipe reaches only $0.244$, the real-\emph{structure} (CJEU)
recipe is the best of the synthetic regimes at $0.340$, and the real-\emph{prose} recipe is $0.265$
--- all far below Presidio. Synthesis closes the \emph{protection} gap (direct-identifier leak), not
the raw detection gap. (Source scorecards: \texttt{res72-cjeu-tab-transfer},
\texttt{res104-xlmr560m-stageb}, \texttt{res102-stageb-transfer},
\texttt{res104-reid-win} in \texttt{klusai-models/docs/}.) This is a citable \emph{negative} on real
external gold and de-risks any over-claim: we lead the direct-identifier-protection axis, not
detection-F1.

\subsection{Scope and honesty}
\label{sec:tab-scope}
We report this on \emph{one} board (TAB, English legal). \texttt{tab\_reid\_leakage} is \emph{our}
metric (competitors publish only detection-F1), but it is grounded in TAB's own external DIRECT/QUASI
annotations, not a self-serving construct; the claim is ``\texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} leads on
direct-identifier protection (re-identification \emph{risk}),'' not ``beats competitors on
detection-F1.'' The training corpus is real legal \emph{structure} with checksum-/format-valid
\emph{synthetic} PII (not real PII). The TAB-leading checkpoint \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} is
\emph{openly released} as \texttt{klusai/kp-deid-xlmr-560m-legal}; the openly-released
\texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m} protector
of Section~\ref{sec:protector} is a \emph{different} model and ranks $7$th on this TAB axis. We make no
``best/strongest/SOTA'' claim. \sotagate{} Breadth (other languages and domains) is the RES-72
multilingual follow-up.

\section{Corroborating Evidence: A Second, Independent Mechanism (Preliminary)}
\label{sec:corroborating}
% PRELIMINARY / IN-PROGRESS (KLU-118). NOT a cited headline. NOT re-identification.
% Sample basis (split:test, 440 ID-subjects / 681 name-subjects) is NOT yet reconciled with the
% board's n=1123, and predates KLU-27. Term: "name-in-context leak" / "residual distinctiveness".
\textbf{Preliminary / in-progress --- corroborating only; not a headline result and not yet
reconciled with the board.} Beyond the deterministic national-ID channel, an early analysis (KLU-118)
measures a second, \emph{independent} channel: a \texttt{PERSON} full name left un-redacted on the
post-detection residual --- a \emph{name-in-context leak}, i.e.\ \emph{residual distinctiveness}, which
we explicitly do \textbf{not} call re-identification. On \texttt{ro-realskeleton-v1}, Presidio leaks
$0\%$ of national IDs but $\sim\!13.7\%$ of names; spaCy shows the inverse profile (high ID leak,
low name leak); kp-deid leaks $0\%$ on both. A per-document $2\times2$ cross-tab indicates the two
channels are largely independent --- different systems fail on different channels --- which is why an
aggregate detection score hides both.

\textbf{Why this is not yet citable.} The name channel's sample basis (\texttt{split:test};
$440$ national-ID subjects and $681$ name subjects) is \emph{not yet reconciled} with the
$n{=}1123$ used for the board-grounded national-ID numbers, and the analysis predates the KLU-27
validation. We therefore present it as corroboration of the \emph{shape} of the dissociation, not as
a measured headline number.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
% Position against TAB, AI4Privacy, MAPA, MultiGraSCCo, MEDDOCAN, GLiNER2-PII, SPY; cite the
% concurrent RAT-Bench HONESTLY (complementary, U.S. demographics, no legal text, no GDPR taxonomy).
% Re-run the KLU-15 prior-art rescan before any broadened public claim (predates 3-ID/legal/Track-C).
This paper builds on the EuroPriv-Bench benchmark and leaderboard~\cite{europrivbench2026}, which
itself subsumes the prior de-identification art (TAB~\cite{pilan2022tab},
AI4Privacy~\cite{ai4privacy_openpii}, MAPA~\cite{mapa_toolkit},
MultiGraSCCo~\cite{multigrascco2026}, MEDDOCAN~\cite{meddocan2019},
GLiNER2-PII~\cite{gliner2pii2026}, SPY~\cite{spy2025}). Our contribution is orthogonal to the
benchmark: a \emph{unified dissociation} result and an open protector.

\textbf{TAB as an external real-data anchor.} The Text Anonymization Benchmark
(TAB)~\cite{pilan2022tab} is the established, peer-reviewed gold standard for legal de-identification:
human-annotated European Court of Human Rights (ECHR) judgments with a published taxonomy that
separates \emph{direct} identifiers (names, case numbers) from \emph{quasi}-identifiers. Because the
synthetic real-skeleton tracks studied above are \texttt{config\_status=dev} (gated on KLU-27 /
RES-77 native-speaker review), we additionally evaluate against TAB as an \emph{external} anchor on
real human-labelled text, where no native-speaker validation gate applies. On TAB's
direct-identifier leak axis (per-subject residual leak after detection; lower is better, over $264$
paired direct-identifier subjects), our protector trained on real CJEU judgment \emph{structure}
(\texttt{kp-cjeu-structure}) attains a $0.095$ direct leak rate, ranking first --- ahead of spaCy
($0.496$), Presidio ($0.500$), GLiNER ($0.599$), \texttt{tabularisai} ($0.625$), GLiNER2 ($0.651$),
and our own \texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m} ($0.674$). The lead is robust across three seeds
($\{0.0947, 0.0985, 0.0985\}$) and statistically significant under a paired bootstrap against the
runner-up spaCy ($\Delta = -0.40$, $95\%$ CI $[-0.477, -0.322]$;
\texttt{reid\_paired\_bootstrap.json}), and it is not an over-tagging artefact (predicted non-\texttt{O}
$9.1\% < $ gold $11.3\%$; over-redaction $4.3\%$). Crucially, the same anchor exhibits the
dissociation this paper documents on an independent gold set: the detection-F1 leader on TAB is
Presidio ($0.589$), yet Presidio leaves $50\%$ of direct identifiers in the residual, and GLiNER2 ---
second on F1 ($0.545$) --- is near-worst on leak ($0.651$). \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} sits mid-pack
on detection-F1 ($0.340$) while leading on protection. We therefore frame the TAB result as
\emph{direct-identifier protection on real legal gold} that bounds re-identification \emph{risk}, and
reserve the term \emph{re-identification} for the deterministic, decode-bearing national-ID channel
(CNP / PESEL / Codice Fiscale) of the main study; the two are distinct channels measured on distinct
data and should not be conflated. The TAB result reuses TAB's own externally-annotated DIRECT/QUASI
identifier types; only the leak-rate aggregation (\texttt{tab\_reid\_leakage}) is ours, and
competitors are scored on TAB detection-F1 with their published configs (reproducible by CI), never
self-reported.

\textbf{Real vs.\ synthetic training, and the synthesis ceiling.} \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} is
trained on real CJEU judgment \emph{structure} (Apache-2.0) populated with synthetic, checksum-valid
PII that is gold by construction; since CJEU $\neq$ ECHR, the TAB evaluation is zero-shot with no
training-set contamination. This pairing also isolates what real data buys: on TAB detection-F1, a
generic-synthetic recipe reaches only $0.244$ and a real-\emph{prose} recipe $0.265$, while the
real-\emph{structure} recipe is best at $0.340$ --- yet all three remain well below Presidio's
$0.589$ detection-F1, underscoring that synthesis closes the protection gap, not the raw detection
gap (source scorecards: \texttt{res104-reid-win}, \texttt{res72-cjeu-tab-transfer},
\texttt{res104-xlmr560m-stageb}, \texttt{res102-stageb-transfer} in \texttt{klusai-models/docs/}). The
openly-released protector evaluated on the national-ID tracks is the \emph{distinct}
\texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m}; \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure} is itself \emph{openly released} as
\texttt{klusai/kp-deid-xlmr-560m-legal} (HF, public). Both protectors are now publicly available.

\textbf{Concurrent re-identification-risk work.} The closest concurrent and independent work is
RAT-Bench~\cite{ratbench2026}, a hosted re-identification-risk leaderboard; it is complementary
rather than overlapping --- it is built on U.S.\ demographics (English/Spanish/Chinese), contains no
legal text, and uses no GDPR-aligned taxonomy, so it does not address either the European national-ID
re-identification setting or the European legal direct-identifier setting studied here.

\textbf{Scope and caveats on the TAB claim.} The TAB direct-identifier ranking is a citable result on
real external gold and is \emph{not} subject to the KLU-27 gate that covers the synthetic tracks.
However, it is bounded: a single board (TAB ECHR, English legal text), the direct-identifier axis
only, and a single protector architecture/recipe. We make no ``best/strongest/SOTA'' claim and no
multilingual or cross-domain generalization claim from it. \textbf{[TODO: re-run the KLU-15 prior-art
rescan before any broadened public claim --- KLU-15 predates the 3-ID / legal / Track-C expansion and
predates this TAB external-anchor result. Confirm ``first \emph{unified}'' still holds, surface any
concurrent legal-de-id or re-id-risk leaderboards, and cite any new concurrent dissociation work,
e.g.\ RAT-Bench, honestly.]}

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}
% Be honest. Synthetic tracks are findings (dev). TAB direct-identifier results are real external gold,
% citable IN SCOPE, but single-board/single-arch/single-language and NOT re-identification rates.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Synthetic-track numbers not yet validated (\texttt{config\_status=dev}).} The
        \emph{national-ID / real-skeleton} numbers are findings on contamination-controlled but
        \texttt{dev}-status \emph{synthetic} tracks, gated on KLU-27. SOTA / ``best protector'' claims
        on these tracks are \textbf{gated on KLU-27} (native-speaker review + inter-annotator
        agreement on \texttt{ro-realskeleton}). (The TAB direct-identifier results are on real
        external gold and are exempt from this gate; see the TAB limitations below.) \sotagate{}
  \item \textbf{Synthetic real-skeleton data.} The real-skeleton tracks are synthetic; the leak-rate
        measures protection on synthetic subjects, not a population re-identification rate against a
        real reference population.
  \item \textbf{TAB result is single-board, single-architecture, single-language.} The
        direct-identifier-protection leadership is measured on one board (TAB, English ECHR legal
        text only) with a single model architecture; breadth across languages/domains is future work
        (RES-72 follow-up). It does \emph{not} establish general de-identification SOTA.
  \item \textbf{TAB direct-identifier $\neq$ deterministic re-identification.} The TAB axis measures
        \emph{direct-identifier} leak (names, case numbers, addresses), a broader and
        non-deterministic notion than the decode-bearing national-ID re-identification used on the
        synthetic tracks. We report it as re-identification-\emph{risk} reduction, not as a measured
        re-identification rate.
  \item \textbf{Two distinct, both-public protectors.} \texttt{kp-cjeu-structure}
        (the \#1 on direct-identifier leak) is \emph{openly released} as
        \texttt{klusai/kp-deid-xlmr-560m-legal}. The openly released protector evaluated on the
        national-ID tracks (\texttt{kp-deid-mdeberta-280m}) is a different (280M) model and ranks
        $7$th on the TAB direct-identifier axis (leak $0.674$). Both are public; we do not conflate
        the two.
  \item \textbf{Single seed for the reported point, 3-seed confirmed.} The published TAB number uses
        seed~0; robustness is confirmed across seeds \{0,1,2\} (direct-leak $\{0.0947,0.0985,0.0985\}$,
        detection-F1 $\{0.340,0.309,0.317\}$). The \texttt{tab\_reid\_leakage} metric is ours
        (competitors publish only detection-F1), though grounded in TAB's own DIRECT/QUASI annotations.
  \item \textbf{Name-in-context channel is preliminary.} Its sample basis is not reconciled with the
        board's $n{=}1123$ and it predates KLU-27 (Section~\ref{sec:corroborating}).
  \item \textbf{No QI-combination metric yet.} We do not yet measure $k$-anonymity-style
        re-identification from \emph{combinations} of quasi-identifiers (the gold lacks binned QI
        tagging); see future work.
  \item \textbf{Prior-art recency.} The ``first \emph{unified}'' positioning must be re-verified
        against KLU-15 before any public claim.
\end{itemize}
