\begin{abstract}
% POSITIONING DISCIPLINE: claim "first to UNIFY", not "first". Cite + subsume prior art.
Privacy-focused NLP for European languages is served by fragmented resources: the Text
Anonymization Benchmark~\cite{pilan2022tab} provides privacy-utility metrics but is
English- and legal-only; AI4Privacy~\cite{ai4privacy_openpii} offers cross-lingual European
\emph{detection} data without re-identification metrics; MAPA~\cite{mapa_toolkit} spans 24 EU
languages and both legal and clinical text but is a detection toolkit, not a leaderboard; and
MultiGraSCCo~\cite{multigrascco2026} is multilingual but clinical-only and translation-based.
Meanwhile the privacy-filter lineage~\cite{openai_privacy_filter_2026,openmed_privacy_filter_ml_2026}
ships strong models with \emph{no} standardized evaluation. We introduce \textbf{EuroPriv-Bench},
the first benchmark to \emph{unify} (a)~European cross-lingual breadth, (b)~both legal and
clinical domains, (c)~one harmonized GDPR-aligned taxonomy, and (d)~re-identification-risk and
privacy-utility metrics in (e)~a single reproducible, openly-licensed, leaderboard-style suite.
We build \emph{on top of} the prior art, re-using their splits and metrics where applicable, and
report a baseline study ranking \texttt{openai/privacy-filter}, OpenMed, \texttt{tabularisai},
GLiNER, and Presidio.
\end{abstract}
